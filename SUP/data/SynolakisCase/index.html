data for Synolakis case runup 1987

This page

First version posted June 2015. Data are supposed to support study on runup of breaking solitary waves on a slope. An addition made for an unbrearking wave September 2016.

The basics

The numerical models in use are a BIM (Boundary integral Model) for full potential theory , a Lagrangian Boussinesq type model, the Globouss tsunami propagation model, and an extended educational Boussinesq model.
Extra information on the output etc. may be found on these pages.

These models are run through a set of shell code snippets given in /hom/geirkp/lab/Erika/genrun and /hom/geirkp/runup/SynolakisCase/genfig. These snippets are executed by the newrun facility.

The data

Data are given in folders, for instance, 
Dataa0.28slope2.884
where amplitude/depth = 0.28, slope angle = 2.884.
Tar files are inflated by 
tar -xz < Dataa0.28slope2.884

All data are dimensionless with d, the depth, as length scale, and time and velocity scaled by means of d and g.

Datafiles are named after the convention 

etacnn_
as in the example etaSerrecn0.1n2400_0180. Here
  • Denotes equation/setting
  • A maximum Courant number
  • resolution (either no. grid points or relative measure in case of BIM)
  • Time interval after reference t=0. Each interval is 0.1

Equation descriptor

The may have one of the values
  • BIM : Boundary integral model (full potential theory)
  • Serre Lagrangian Serre-type equations where may be
  • Serre Lagrangian Serre-type equations where may be
    • void : A moderately nonlinear version
    • reg : Fully nonlinear, no singularity when contact angle is 90 deg.
    • sing : Fully nonlinear, singular when contact angle is 90 deg.:
    • <..>ho-lin : with ... as lin, reg or sing. First three options with a Madsen and Scaeffer type correction term.
  • Pg Peregrine type equations in eduactonal models where may be
    • void: Just standard Peregrine equation
    • lin : linear dispersive Peregrine
    • ho0 : Madsen and Schaeffer
    • SeS0 : Madsen and Schaeffer with Serre initial soliton
    • LSW : LSW equation
  • Glob: Peregrine from GloBouss

Some results

The BIM solution and breaking

The evolution of the surface for slope 2.885 deg and amplitude 0.28 times the depth
  • An ampliude of 0.8 times depth is reached at t=14.8 , when the crest is at 8.17 with h=0.411 (snippet BIMh0.8).
    Corresponding data for Serre solution is t=14.7, position x=8.175 (snippet Serreh0.8)
  • A vertical front appears at t=18.6, x=4.02, y=0.358, when the crest is at x=4.09 and y=0.414.
  • The breaking is still a little sensitive to grid refinement.
